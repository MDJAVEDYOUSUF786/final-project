Sanga Shiva Temple

Sanga, the destination broadly popular as a sacred Hindu pilgrimage destination among the Nepalese, is located at the border of Bhaktapur and Kavrepalanchowk district. It is located in about 20 kilometers away from Kathmandu. The Kailashnath Mahadev statue gazing towards the city of Kathmandu valley from the hilltop is the most mesmerizing attraction of Sanga. It is considered as the tallest statue of Lord Shiva. The sanga mahadev height is 144ft. This statue is a pride of many Nepalese people because Lord Shiva is considered a very powerful and sacred god amongst the Hindus. The height of sanga is 1480m from the sea level. The total population of Sanga is 6,121 among the 1,305 individual households, according to Nepal Census 2011. The major population of Sanga is of Newari culture, where numbers of Brahmin, Chetri, Tamang and some other castes of people also stay in different communities. If you want to visit Sanga as a pilgrim we recommend visiting Sanga during the month of Falgun at the occasion of Shivaratri. Thousands of people go to visit Kailashnath Temple during Mahashivaratri to worship Lord Shiva. The Teej festival during the Shrawan month also attracts lots of visitors to this marvelous destination.
